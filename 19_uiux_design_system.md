# 19. UI/UX Design System

## 1. Goal
To create a premium, high-energy, and cohesive visual language that feels like a modern video game rather than a dry educational tool.

## 2. Visual Identity

### Color Palette
- **Primary (Action):** `Vibrant Purple (#7B2CBF)` / `Electric Blue (#3A86FF)`
- **Success (Gamification):** `Emerald Green (#06D6A0)`
- **Warning/Error:** `Bright Orange (#FF9F1C)` / `Rose Red (#EF476F)`
- **Neutral:** `Soft Gray (#F8F9FA)` for text, `Deep Slate (#212529)` for dark mode backgrounds.
- **Accents:** Neon highlights for buttons and active states.

### Typography
- **Headlines:** `Quicksand` or `Fredoka One` (Rounded, friendly, highly legible).
- **Body Copy:** `Inter` or `Montserrat` (Clean, professional).

---

## 3. UI/UX Principles

### The "WOW" Factor (Visual Excellence)
- **Glassmorphism:** Sublte blur effects on teacher dashboards to feel premium.
- **Gradients:** Smooth, multi-stop gradients for background and buttons.
- **Micro-Animations:** 
    - Buttons "Squish" when pressed.
    - Stars "Pop" and fly toward the counter.
    - Avatars have a subtle "Breathe" idle animation.

### "Zero UI" for Classroom Board
- **Constraint:** No mouse pointers or small text.
- **Design:** Everything must be legible from 5 meters away. Use giant fonts (48pt+) and high-contrast icons.

### Mobile-First for Remote/Student App
- **Thumb Zones:** All primary actions (Spin Wheel, Record) located in the easy-to-reach bottom third of the screen.
- **Haptic Feedback:** Vibrations for correct/wrong answers to enhance the "Game" feel.

---

## 4. Component Tokens
- **Elevation:** Use soft shadows to distinguish layers (Cards over Backgrounds).
- **Rounded Corners:** Consistent `border-radius: 16px` for a modern, kid-friendly look.
- **Active State:** Glowing borders or "Pulse" animations for elements requiring attention.
